\section{Related Work}
\paragraph{Federated Graph Learning (FGL)} Recent works such as \textit{FedAvg} and \textit{APFL} attempt to handle data heterogeneity by personalizing local models. However, as shown in previous studies on the PubMed dataset, these methods often struggle with minority node accuracy.

\paragraph{Graph Alignment} \textit{REGAL} introduced the concept of structural identity using $k$-hop degree descriptors. By projecting these descriptors into a shared latent space via \textit{xNetMF}, nodes can be matched across networks with sub-quadratic complexity. Our work extends this to a federated context.
